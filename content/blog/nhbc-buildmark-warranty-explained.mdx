The NHBC Buildmark warranty is the most widely used new build warranty in the UK, covering around 80% of all new homes. If you're buying a new build in England, Scotland, or Wales, there's a very good chance your property is covered by Buildmark — but most buyers have only a vague idea of what that actually means in practice.

This guide explains the Buildmark warranty clearly: what it covers, what it doesn't, how long protection lasts, and what you need to do to make sure you can actually use it when you need it.

## What Is NHBC Buildmark?

NHBC (National House Building Council) is the UK's largest provider of new home warranties and building control services. Its Buildmark warranty is a 10-year protection scheme that covers new build homes from the date of legal completion.

Buildmark provides two distinct types of protection:

1. **Builder liability** in years 1 and 2 — the builder is responsible for fixing defects
2. **NHBC insurance** from year 3 onwards — NHBC covers structural defects if the builder can't or won't fix them

Every home registered with NHBC receives a Buildmark policy document at completion. This document sets out the coverage, limitations, and how to make a claim. Your solicitor should provide this to you as part of your completion pack.

## Buildmark Coverage: Year by Year

### Years 1 and 2 — Builder's Liability

During the first two years after legal completion, your builder is required by the Buildmark scheme to put right any defects they are responsible for. This is the broadest coverage period and covers:

- Plasterwork defects and cracking beyond settlement norms
- Plumbing faults — leaks, poor pressure, drainage issues
- Electrical defects — faulty wiring, sockets not working correctly
- Joinery defects — sticking doors, poorly hung windows, warped frames
- Tiling defects — lippage, hollow tiles, grout failures
- Heating system faults — boilers, radiators, controls
- Roofing defects visible from ground level or in the loft space
- External brickwork and render defects
- Kitchen fitting defects

**Your responsibility:** Report defects in writing to your builder. NHBC's role in years 1–2 is as a backstop — if your builder refuses to fix something or goes out of business, NHBC will step in.

### Years 3–10 — NHBC Insurance

From year 3 onwards, Buildmark becomes an insurance product rather than a builder warranty. NHBC covers you against:

- **Physical damage** caused by a defect in the home's structure
- **Contaminated land** discovered after completion
- **Builder insolvency** — if the builder went out of business before completing any defects from years 1–2

Importantly, from year 3 onwards, coverage is limited to defects that cause **physical damage**. Minor defects that don't cause damage — even if they were present from the start — are generally not covered after year 2.

### What Is Not Covered

Buildmark has significant exclusions that catch many buyers off guard:

- **Normal wear and tear** — carpets, paintwork, and decorative finishes are not covered.
- **Cosmetic defects after year 2** — hairline cracks in plasterwork, minor settlement, surface finishes.
- **Defects not reported in writing in years 1–2** — verbal complaints are not sufficient.
- **Owner-caused damage** — any alterations, modifications, or damage caused by you or your contractors.
- **Condensation** — unless caused by a defect in the structure.
- **Appliances and white goods** — these have their own manufacturer warranties.
- **Boundary fences** — unless they are structural retaining walls.
- **Driveways and garden areas** unless specified as covered in your contract.

## How to Make an NHBC Buildmark Claim

### In Years 1–2: Report to Your Builder First

1. Document the defect with photographs and a clear written description.
2. Send your defect report in writing to your builder's customer care team. Email is fine — keep a copy.
3. The builder should acknowledge within 7 days and arrange repair within a reasonable period (typically 30–60 days).
4. If the builder doesn't respond or refuses to repair, contact NHBC to escalate.

### Escalating to NHBC

If your builder fails to address a defect:
- Call NHBC Resolution on **0344 633 1000** or use the online portal at **nhbc.co.uk**
- NHBC will review your case and contact the builder on your behalf
- If unresolved, NHBC will appoint an independent inspector

### In Years 3–10: Claim Direct with NHBC

From year 3, you claim directly with NHBC via their Resolution team. You'll need:
- Your Buildmark policy number (on your original documentation)
- Evidence of the defect (photographs, reports)
- Evidence that the defect was not caused by wear and tear or owner action

## The Most Important Thing Buyers Get Wrong

The single most common reason NHBC claims fail is **verbal reporting**. If you told your site manager about a defect over coffee or at a show home event, that doesn't count. You must report in writing — email, letter, or via your builder's online portal.

If it's not in writing, it doesn't exist as far as NHBC is concerned.

The second most common mistake is **letting year 2 lapse without a follow-up inspection**. Settlement cracks, water staining behind furniture, and slow plumbing leaks often take 12–18 months to become visible. Do a full inspection at 11 months and submit any new items before your first year ends. Do it again at 23 months before year 2 ends.

## 5 Actions to Protect Your NHBC Entitlements

1. **Get your Buildmark policy document from your solicitor.** Know your policy number and completion date.
2. **Do a full snagging inspection at handover** and submit in writing within 7 days.
3. **Set calendar reminders** for month 11 and month 23 — repeat your inspection and submit any new items.
4. **Keep every email and photograph** related to defects. This evidence is essential if you need to escalate.
5. **Report immediately** if you discover a water leak or roof defect — don't wait for your follow-up inspection.

## NHBC vs Premier Guarantee vs LABC Warranty

Buildmark is the most widely used scheme but not the only one. Some UK new builds use:
- **Premier Guarantee** — a similar 10-year structural warranty from MD Insurance Services
- **LABC New Home Warranty** — backed by local authority building control
- **Checkmate Castle 10** — another FCA-regulated warranty provider

All of these work on broadly similar principles — builder liability in years 1–2, insurance cover for structural defects in years 3–10. The claims process and exclusions differ, so always read your specific policy document.
