If you've just taken the keys to a new build home in Ireland, or you're days away from completion, a thorough snag list is one of the most important things you can do to protect your investment. Builders are legally required to repair defects reported within your HomeBond warranty period — but only if you find them and document them properly.

This guide walks you through every room, every system, and every surface you should check. It's based on the same methodology professional snagging surveyors use — adapted so you can do it yourself.

## What Is a Snag List?

A snag list (also called a snagging list or defect list) is a written record of all incomplete or defective work in a new build property. It covers everything from cosmetic issues like paint blemishes to structural concerns like cracks in plasterwork or missing insulation.

In Ireland, builders are required under the HomeBond warranty scheme to rectify defects reported within the warranty period. Without a formal, documented snag list, you have very little leverage to force repairs.

## When Should You Do Your Snag List?

The ideal time is **before completion** — before you sign the final paperwork and hand over the balance of your purchase price. However, many buyers only get access to the property at handover. In that case, do your snag list on the day you receive the keys and submit it to your builder in writing within 7 days.

Under HomeBond, you also have the right to report defects:
- **Year 1 and 2:** Non-structural defects (finishes, fittings, plumbing, electrics)
- **Years 1–5:** Water ingress and dampness
- **Years 1–10:** Structural defects

Don't wait — defects are far easier and cheaper to fix when the builder's crews are still on site.

## Room-by-Room Snag List Checklist

### External and Approach

Start outside before you enter the property. Walk the full perimeter.

1. **Brickwork and render:** Look for cracks, gaps in mortar joints, uneven courses, and areas where render is hollow or detached (tap gently).
2. **Roof:** From the ground, check that ridge tiles are straight, no slates are cracked or slipping, and all valleys and hips are properly dressed.
3. **Windows and doors:** Check all external windows open, close and lock correctly. Check weatherstripping is intact. Look for gaps between frames and brickwork.
4. **Gutters and downpipes:** Ensure they are properly aligned and secured. Look for any gaps at joints.
5. **Driveway and paths:** Check for cracks, trip hazards, and poor drainage slope. Water should drain away from the house.
6. **Garden:** Check boundary walls are plumb. Fencing posts should be secure and vertical. If a garden has been seeded, check topsoil depth is adequate.
7. **Soffits and fascias:** Should be level, correctly fixed, and painted with no runs or misses.

### Front Door and Hall

1. The front door should hang perfectly and swing freely without sticking. The latch should engage on the first swing.
2. Check the letterbox operates correctly.
3. Inspect all plasterwork for cracks — pay particular attention to corners and around door frames.
4. Test the light switch and verify it operates the correct fitting.
5. Check skirting boards are correctly fitted with no gaps at corners or wall joints.
6. Inspect flooring for lippage (uneven tile edges), grout consistency, and any damaged tiles.

### Sitting Room / Lounge

1. Test every socket in the room with a socket tester.
2. Check all light fittings are correctly installed and flush with the ceiling.
3. Inspect coving and cornicing for gaps and inconsistent mitres.
4. Check for paint misses or poor cut-in at ceiling/wall junctions — best viewed in natural light from an angle.
5. Check the chimney breast (if present) for cracks and ensure the fireplace surround is correctly secured.
6. Test any TV aerial or CAT5 data socket outlets.

### Kitchen

The kitchen is where builders often cut corners. Be thorough.

1. Open and close every cabinet door and drawer. They should move smoothly and align perfectly.
2. Check handles are straight and correctly positioned.
3. Inspect worktops for scratches, burns, or poor joints at mitre cuts.
4. Check the splashback tiling for alignment, lippage, and consistent grout.
5. Run the kitchen tap — hot water should reach within 30 seconds. Check under the sink for leaks immediately.
6. Test the extractor fan on all settings — it should draw air effectively.
7. Test the dishwasher connection and run a cycle if appliances are fitted.
8. Check integrated appliances open and close correctly and are level.
9. Inspect the plinth boards — they should be level and consistent.

### Bathrooms and En-Suites

1. Check all tiling: alignment, lippage, grout consistency, and silicon joints at floor/wall junctions. Missing or cracked grout allows water ingress.
2. Flush every toilet. The cistern should refill within 60 seconds.
3. Run the shower — test both pressure and drainage speed. The tray should empty without pooling.
4. Check shower screens open and close correctly and the seals are intact.
5. Test the bathroom extractor fan — it should come on with the light and run on for at least 15 minutes.
6. Inspect the basin and vanity unit for secure fixing and even gaps.
7. Check bath panels are securely fitted and access panels (if any) can be opened.

### Bedrooms

1. Test every socket and check placement matches the specification.
2. Open and close built-in wardrobe doors — check they're plumb and the hinges are correctly adjusted.
3. Check radiators heat fully. TRVs (thermostatic radiator valves) should turn smoothly.
4. Inspect windows: they should open on a restrictive latch and lock with a key.
5. Check the ceiling for paint uniformity — spray shadows and uneven application are common in bedrooms.

### Attic and Roof Space

If accessible, inspect the attic:

1. Insulation should cover the full floor area with no gaps. Minimum 300mm in Ireland under building regulations.
2. Check for any signs of water ingress — stains, damp timber, or mold.
3. Verify ventilation is present — soffit vents should be clear and not blocked by insulation.
4. Check that any water tank is lagged (insulated) to prevent freezing.

### Heating and Plumbing

1. Run the central heating for a full cycle and bleed any radiators that don't heat fully.
2. Check the boiler pressure gauge — it should read 1–1.5 bar when cold.
3. Test the programmer/thermostat to confirm zones respond correctly.
4. Check all hot water cylinder controls if a cylinder is fitted.
5. Verify the stopcock location and test it opens and closes freely.

## How to Document Your Snag List

A poorly documented snag list is easy for a builder to dismiss. To make yours impossible to ignore:

- **Photograph every defect.** Include a wide shot showing location and a close-up showing detail. Use natural light or a torch.
- **Note the room and exact location** ("Master bedroom, south wall, 400mm from corner").
- **Describe the defect clearly** — "Grout missing from joint between floor and wall tiles at south-east corner of en-suite shower tray."
- **Number each item** — builders respond better to a numbered list than a block of text.
- **Send it in writing** — email with read receipt to your site manager and customer care contact.

## The HomeBond Warranty: What You're Entitled To

HomeBond is Ireland's structural guarantee scheme for new homes. Key coverage:

- **Years 1–2:** The builder is responsible for repairing all defects reported in writing during this period.
- **Years 1–5:** Water ingress is covered by HomeBond if the builder fails to repair it.
- **Years 1–10:** Structural defects (foundations, walls, roof structure) are covered by HomeBond after year 2.

To claim under HomeBond, you must:
1. Report the defect to your builder in writing first.
2. Allow a reasonable period for repair (usually 30 days).
3. If unresolved, contact HomeBond directly at homebond.ie.

Always keep copies of your snag list, all correspondence with the builder, and any photographic evidence.

## Tips From Professional Snagging Surveyors

1. **Do it in daylight.** Artificial light misses a lot. Do your inspection on a bright day with natural light flooding each room.
2. **Bring a socket tester.** Available for under €10. Plug it into every socket — it instantly tells you if there's a wiring fault.
3. **Take a spirit level.** Useful for checking tiling, kitchen units, and skirting boards.
4. **Tap tiles.** A hollow sound indicates a poor adhesive bond — common on large-format tiles.
5. **Run all water for five minutes.** Most plumbing leaks only appear under sustained use, not a quick turn of the tap.

## Common Defects Found in Irish New Builds

Based on inspections conducted across Ireland, the most frequently found defects are:

- Uneven or cracked plasterwork
- Missing, incomplete, or poorly applied grout
- Kitchen cabinet doors misaligned or hinges not adjusted
- Radiators with airlocks (not bleeding correctly)
- Gaps between skirting boards and walls
- Incomplete or missing attic insulation
- External brickwork with mortar gaps
- Poorly sealed window frames
- Paint misses in corners and ceiling edges
- Damaged or stained worktops (often from construction)

## Submitting Your Snag List to Your Builder

Once your snag list is complete:

1. Email it to your site manager and your builder's customer care team.
2. Keep a timestamped copy — note the date sent.
3. Follow up in writing after 14 days if you haven't received a response.
4. Builders in Ireland are typically given 30 days to address each defect.
5. If your builder is unresponsive, contact HomeBond or CIRI (Construction Industry Register Ireland).
