Your new home handover inspection is the single most important walkthrough you'll ever do. What you document on that day — and in the days immediately following — determines what your builder is legally obligated to fix at no cost to you.

This checklist is designed to guide you through every area of an Australian new build home at handover, room by room. Use it alongside SnapSnag's digital guided inspection to ensure you don't miss a single item.

## Before You Arrive: What to Bring

- **Phone or camera** — for photographing everything (not just defects)
- **Socket/power point tester** — available at Bunnings or Jaycar for under $15
- **Spirit level** — for checking tiling, skirting, and kitchen benchtops
- **Torch or head torch** — for roof spaces, under-sink areas, and dark cupboards
- **Notepad or the SnapSnag app** — to record every item as you find it
- **Your building contract and specifications** — so you can verify what was agreed versus what's been delivered
- **A spare 30 minutes** beyond what your builder has scheduled

## Exterior and Site

Walk the full perimeter before entering the home.

**Roof:**
- Check from ground level that all tiles or metal roof sheets are undamaged and aligned
- No cracked, slipped, or missing tiles
- Flashings at all wall junctions, chimneys, and penetrations should be neatly dressed and sealed
- Gutters should be aligned and slope correctly toward downpipes
- Downpipes should be connected and discharge to the stormwater system

**External Walls:**
- Rendered surfaces: no cracks, bubbling, or hollow areas (tap gently — hollow sound indicates poor adhesion)
- Brick homes: mortar joints should be fully filled and consistently finished
- Cladding: no gaps between boards, all trim pieces correctly fixed

**Windows and External Doors:**
- Open, close, and lock every external window and door
- Check flyscreen frames are undamaged and screens are fully intact
- Weatherstripping should be complete with no gaps
- Check window reveals are fully plastered or rendered

**Garage:**
- Electric garage door operates correctly in both directions
- Auto-reverse safety mechanism works (place a piece of timber in the path — it must reverse)
- Garage floor is free of major cracks and properly drained
- No gaps at the base or sides of the garage door

**Driveway, Paths and Landscaping:**
- No cracks or settlement in driveway or paths
- Paving slopes away from house for drainage
- Garden areas match the agreed specification
- Fencing is plumb, posts are secure, gates swing and latch correctly
- Any retaining walls are correctly drained and free of cracks

## Internal — Living Areas

**Living Room / Lounge:**
- Inspect walls and ceiling in natural light for paint finish quality, cracks, and misses
- Test all power points with a tester
- Check all light switches operate the correct fittings
- Skirting boards should be flush against the wall with tight mitred corners
- Cornices or ceiling details should have consistent gaps and no cracks
- Inspect floor surfaces for damage, lippage (tile edges), or gaps in joinery flooring

**Dining Room:**
- Same checks as living room
- Verify data and TV points are correctly installed and in agreed positions
- Check pendant light positions match the specification

## Kitchen

The kitchen deserves particular attention — it's where the most defects are found.

- Open and close every cabinet door and drawer at least twice
- Doors should align perfectly when closed — no step between adjacent doors
- Drawer runners should operate smoothly and not drop at the front
- Benchtop mitre joints should be flush with no visible gap
- Check benchtop surface for scratches, water marks, or damage from construction
- Splashback tiling: consistent grout lines, no lippage, fully sealed at benchtop edge
- Under-sink area: run the tap for two minutes and inspect every joint and connection for drips
- Test the sink waste — it should drain freely without slow draining
- Run the rangehood on all settings — check it extracts and doesn't rattle
- Test all appliances if fitted (oven, cooktop, dishwasher)
- Plinth boards should be level and consistently gapped at the floor

## Bathrooms and Ensuite

- Check all tiling: alignment, lippage, consistent grout width. Tap each tile — hollow sound means poor adhesion.
- Silicon sealant at all bath/shower tray/basin edges must be continuous with no gaps
- Flush every toilet — time the cistern refill (should be under 90 seconds)
- Run every shower: check pressure, temperature reach, and drainage speed
- Check shower screens open smoothly, close fully, and seals are intact
- Test every tap — hot water should arrive within 30 seconds
- Check exhaust fans operate and continue to run after the light is switched off
- Vanity units should be securely fixed to the wall and level
- Check toilet suite is secure — no movement when sat on

## Bedrooms

- Test every power point
- Built-in robes: sliding doors should glide without lifting out of the track, hinged doors should hang plumb
- Check bedroom doors close fully and latch cleanly
- Radiators or reverse-cycle units should be correctly installed and operational
- Inspect windows — open, close, and lock. Flyscreens should be fitted and undamaged.
- Check ceiling fans if fitted — all speeds, no wobble

## Laundry

- Check hot and cold taps are plumbed correctly (hot on left)
- Test the drain — run a bucket of water and verify drainage rate
- Verify the washing machine connection points are in the correct position
- Check any overhead cabinet doors and shelves

## Garage Interior

- Power points should be tested
- Light fitting should work
- Check for any cracking in the slab or walls
- If there's a door from garage to house, check it is a fire-rated door with self-closing hinges (required by building code)

## Roof Space (if safely accessible)

- Insulation should be fully laid with no gaps
- No visible moisture, water staining, or mould on roof timbers
- Ventilation present (sarking, ridge vents, or eave vents)
- All bracing and structural members appear as built — nothing cut, notched, or altered

## Mechanical Systems

**Hot Water:**
- Check the system type matches specification (solar, heat pump, gas, electric)
- Temperature should be set to 60°C minimum
- Pressure relief valve should discharge over a drain or tundish

**Ducted Air Conditioning:**
- Test in both heat and cool modes
- Every outlet should deliver airflow — check with your hand
- Return air grilles should be accessible and not blocked

**Electrical:**
- Consumer board should be correctly labelled
- All circuits accounted for and identified
- Safety switches (RCDs) should be present on all circuits

## 5 Things Most Australian Buyers Miss at Handover

1. **The garage door auto-reverse.** It's a legal safety requirement and builders often forget to test it.
2. **Hot water temperature.** Too low increases legionella risk. Ask your builder what temperature it's set to.
3. **The fly screens.** Easy to miss — go around every window and push each screen. Poorly fitted ones will pop out.
4. **Under-sink plumbing.** Run water for two minutes, then crouch down and look for any drips at every connection.
5. **External drainage slope.** Get low and look along the driveway and paths — they must slope away from the house.
