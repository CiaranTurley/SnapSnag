A professional snagging survey in Ireland costs between €190 and €500 depending on the surveyor and the size of the property. For many first-time buyers who have just stretched their budget to buy a new home, that's a significant additional expense — especially when it comes on top of solicitor fees, stamp duty, and moving costs.

The good news is that you can do your own snag list to a professional standard if you know what to look for, how to document what you find, and how to present it to your builder. This guide shows you exactly how.

## DIY vs Professional Snagging: What's the Difference?

A professional snagging surveyor brings:
- Specialist equipment (thermal imaging, moisture meters, socket testers)
- Construction knowledge to identify structural or regulatory concerns
- Independence and authority that builders sometimes take more seriously

What you can do yourself with guidance:
- Every visual check — which represents about 80% of a professional survey
- Functional testing of every fitting, socket, door, window, and appliance
- Photograph and document every defect to a professional standard
- Generate a formatted report suitable for your builder and HomeBond

**For the vast majority of new builds, a careful DIY inspection — guided by a proper checklist — will catch the same defects a professional survey would.** The exceptions are if you suspect serious structural issues, have a large or complex property, or your builder has a history of disputes.

## What You'll Need

Before you start, gather:

- **A phone** with a good camera (or a digital camera)
- **A socket tester** — available in any hardware store for €5–€10. Plug it into every socket to instantly check wiring.
- **A torch or head torch** — for attics, under-sink areas, and poorly lit corners
- **A spirit level** — useful for checking tiling, skirting, and kitchen units
- **A marble or ball** — roll it on tiled floors to check for draining slopes or lippage
- **A notepad** — or use SnapSnag on your phone to capture everything digitally as you go
- **Comfortable shoes** — you'll be on your feet for 2–3 hours

## When to Do Your Inspection

**Before completion is best.** If your builder allows a pre-completion walk-through, take it — this is your strongest position. Defects found before you sign the final paperwork are the easiest to get fixed.

If you only get access at handover (key collection day), do your inspection that day and submit your snag list within 7 days in writing. Under your HomeBond warranty, you can still submit non-structural defects in years 1 and 2, but the sooner the better — builders are far more responsive when their crew is still on site.

## Step-by-Step: How to Do Your Snag List

### Step 1: Photograph Before You Enter

Before you go inside, walk the full external perimeter and photograph:
- Every elevation (front, rear, both sides)
- The roof from the ground — particularly ridges, valleys, and any chimneys
- The driveway, pathways, and garden boundaries
- All windows and external doors
- Gutters, downpipes, soffits, and fascias

These photos establish the baseline condition before you (or anyone else) have been in and out.

### Step 2: Work Room by Room, Top to Bottom

The most common mistake DIY snagging makes is jumping around randomly and missing things. Work systematically:

1. Enter each room and stand in the centre.
2. Look at the ceiling first. Check for paint uniformity, cracks, poorly trimmed coving, and light fitting alignment.
3. Work around the walls clockwise. Check plasterwork, skirting, architraves, and switches.
4. Check windows (open, close, lock, check weatherstripping).
5. Test every socket.
6. Check the floor — lippage, damaged tiles or laminate, gaps at skirting.
7. Test any specialist fittings (radiator TRV, extractor fan, light dimmer).
8. Photograph every defect as you find it.

### Step 3: Test All Plumbing

For every bathroom, kitchen, and utility room:
- Run every tap for at least two minutes. Check for adequate hot water pressure and temperature.
- Flush every toilet and watch the cistern refill — should complete in under 60 seconds.
- Run the shower and observe drainage speed — no pooling.
- Check under every sink immediately after running the tap for drips or moisture.

### Step 4: Run the Heating System

Turn the boiler on and set the thermostat to max. Let the system run for 30 minutes, then check:
- Does every radiator heat fully? Cold spots at the top indicate air — bleed it.
- Is the boiler pressure holding at 1–1.5 bar?
- Does the programmer control zones correctly?

### Step 5: Test the Electrics

With your socket tester, plug into every single socket in the property — including external sockets, garage sockets, and loft sockets. A green light means correct wiring. Any other indication means a fault.

Also test:
- Every light switch (does it operate the right fitting?)
- The consumer unit — are all MCBs labelled correctly?
- Any outdoor lights and PIR sensors

### Step 6: Check the Attic

If you can safely access the attic:
- Is insulation covering the full floor area with no gaps? Minimum depth is 300mm.
- Are there any signs of moisture, staining, or mould on the timber?
- Is there adequate ventilation (clear soffit vents)?
- Is any water tank properly lagged (insulated)?

### Step 7: Document Everything

For each defect found:
- Take a wide shot showing the room/location
- Take a close-up clearly showing the defect
- Note: room name, location within room, description of defect

## 5 Tips That Make Your Snag List Impossible to Ignore

1. **Number every item.** Builders respond to numbered lists — item 47 is easier to track than "the crack in the kitchen."
2. **Use specific locations.** "North wall of master bedroom, 600mm from window frame" beats "bedroom wall."
3. **Send via email, not verbally.** Your snag list needs a timestamp and a paper trail. Never hand it over in person without a written copy.
4. **Follow up in writing.** If you haven't had a response within 14 days, send a follow-up referencing your original submission.
5. **Reference HomeBond.** Mentioning HomeBond in your covering letter signals to your builder that you know your rights and are prepared to escalate.

## What Happens After You Submit

Once you've submitted your snag list:

- **Day 1–7:** Expect an acknowledgement from your builder's customer care team.
- **Days 7–30:** Site crew should be in to address items. Some builders schedule a formal "snag completion" walk-through.
- **Day 30+:** If items remain unresolved, issue a formal written notice referencing HomeBond.
- **If unresolved after 60 days:** Contact HomeBond directly at homebond.ie. They will appoint an independent inspector.

Keep all correspondence. A documented paper trail is your strongest asset if a dispute develops.
