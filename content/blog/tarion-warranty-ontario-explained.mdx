If you're buying a new home in Ontario, Tarion is one of the most important organisations you'll deal with after closing. Yet most buyers know almost nothing about it until they have a problem. Understanding the Tarion warranty system before you take possession — not after — can save you thousands of dollars and months of frustration.

This guide explains the Tarion warranty clearly: what it covers, the critical warranty list timelines, how to file a claim, and what happens when your builder won't comply.

## What Is Tarion?

Tarion Warranty Corporation (now operating as Tarion under the New Home Construction Licensing Act, 2017) administers Ontario's new home warranty and buyer protection program. It is funded by builders through registration and warranty fees — not by the Ontario government, though it operates under provincial legislation.

Every new home built in Ontario by a licensed builder must be enrolled in the Tarion warranty program. When you buy a new home, your purchase is protected regardless of whether your individual builder remains in business.

Tarion provides two layers of protection:
1. **Builder warranty obligations** — legal requirements imposed on your builder
2. **Tarion warranty insurance** — coverage provided directly by Tarion if the builder fails to meet their obligations

## Tarion Warranty Coverage Periods

Ontario's new home warranty is structured in layers, with different coverage periods for different types of defects:

### Deposit Protection

Before your home is even built, Tarion protects your deposit:
- Up to $100,000 for freehold homes
- Up to $20,000 for condominiums (separate HCRA rules apply)
- Protects against builder insolvency, bankruptcy, or failure to complete

### 1-Year Warranty

Starts on the date of possession. Covers:
- Defects in work and materials
- Violations of Ontario Building Code
- Water penetration through basement or foundation walls
- Unauthorized substitutions (builder changed specified materials without your agreement)

### 2-Year Warranty

Covers specific categories beyond year 1:
- Water penetration through the building envelope (cladding, windows, doors, roof)
- Defects in the electrical, plumbing, and heating delivery and distribution systems
- Defects in exterior cladding that cause detachment or damage
- Violations of Ontario Building Code affecting the structural, electrical, plumbing, or HVAC systems

### 7-Year Structural Warranty

Covers major structural defects for 7 years from the possession date:
- Failure of load-bearing parts of the home (foundations, beams, walls)
- Damage to the home caused by a structural defect
- Any defect that is hazardous to health or safety

## The Warranty Lists: Your Most Important Dates

Tarion's warranty system is built around **written warranty lists** that you submit at specific times. These lists trigger your builder's legal obligation to respond and repair.

### PDI Form (Pre-Delivery Inspection)

Completed before or on your possession date. Use it to document:
- Incomplete work
- Damage visible at possession
- Items that don't match the agreed specification

The PDI form is signed by both you and a builder representative. Keep a copy. Items on the PDI form are covered under the 1-year warranty.

### 30-Day Warranty List

Submit within **30 days of possession** via your MyHome account at tarion.com. Covers:
- Items missed at the PDI
- Defects that appeared in the first month after possession
- Anything on the PDI that the builder said they would fix but hasn't yet

**This is a critical deadline.** Missing the 30-day window doesn't eliminate your warranty rights, but it does remove items from the 30-day list — they can still be covered under the year-end list if they appear later.

### Year-End Warranty List

Submit between **days 270 and 365** of possession (the final 90 days of year 1). Covers:
- New defects that have appeared during the year
- Any items from the 30-day list that haven't been fully repaired
- Settlement cracks that have become visible over the year
- Heating and plumbing issues that emerged in winter

**Tip:** Schedule a full re-inspection of your home at month 10. This gives you time to photograph and document everything before the year-end list submission window opens.

### Year-Two Warranty List

Submit between **months 18 and 24** of possession. Covers:
- Water penetration through the building envelope
- Mechanical system defects (electrical, plumbing, HVAC)
- Ontario Building Code violations not previously addressed

After month 24, most non-structural coverage expires. The 7-year structural warranty continues.

## How to Submit a Tarion Warranty List

1. Create a MyHome account at **tarion.com**
2. Your home should already be registered — you'll receive a certificate of registration from Tarion at closing
3. Log in and navigate to "Submit a Warranty Form"
4. Complete each item with:
   - Room/location
   - Description of the defect
   - Date first noticed
   - Photographs (strongly recommended)
5. Submit before the relevant deadline

Tarion will send the warranty list to your builder, who has a defined period to respond:
- **Non-emergency items:** 30 days to respond, 120 days to repair (though most repairs should happen sooner)
- **Emergency items** (life safety, no heat in winter, major water infiltration): 24–48 hour response expected

## What Happens If Your Builder Won't Repair?

This is where Tarion's insurance function comes in.

1. Submit your warranty list through MyHome (establishes the official record)
2. If the builder doesn't respond or disputes your claim, contact Tarion directly
3. Tarion will assign a Customer Relations Manager to your file
4. If warranted, Tarion will conduct a field inspection
5. If Tarion determines the defect is covered, they will issue a conciliation order requiring the builder to repair
6. If the builder still doesn't comply, **Tarion will arrange and fund the repair** and pursue the builder for the cost

Tarion has financial authority to repair homes up to the coverage limits when builders fail to comply. This is the key protection the scheme provides — you're not left chasing a builder through the courts.

## 5 Actions to Get the Most From Your Tarion Warranty

1. **Register your home with Tarion before possession.** Your builder should register your home, but verify this happened. If your home isn't registered, Tarion can't help you.

2. **Do a thorough PDI.** Use SnapSnag or a comprehensive checklist. The more you document on the PDI form, the clearer the record of what existed at possession.

3. **Set calendar reminders for each list deadline.** Month 1, month 9 (re-inspection), month 10 (submit year-end list), month 18 (final re-inspection), month 22 (submit 2-year list).

4. **Photograph defects with your phone the day you notice them.** The timestamp on the photo is evidence of when the defect appeared — important if a builder disputes whether it was present before possession.

5. **Submit everything through MyHome — not by email to your builder.** Tarion's system creates the official record. Email to your builder alone is not a warranty list submission.

## Tarion Outside Ontario

Tarion only operates in Ontario. Other provinces have their own new home warranty programs:

- **British Columbia:** BC Housing Homeowner Protection Office — 2/5/10 warranty coverage
- **Alberta:** Alberta New Home Warranty Program — 1/2/5/10 coverage
- **Saskatchewan:** Provincial Home Warranty Program
- **Manitoba:** Manitoba New Home Warranty Program
- **Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, PEI:** Provincial programs with varying coverage

If you're outside Ontario, check your provincial warranty program for the equivalent warranty lists and deadlines — the principles are similar but the specifics differ.
