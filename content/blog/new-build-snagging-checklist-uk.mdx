New builds in the UK have a well-documented snagging problem. Research from the Home Builders Federation shows that the vast majority of new build buyers report defects after moving in — and many of those defects could have been identified and fixed before completion if buyers knew what to look for.

This room-by-room snagging checklist gives you everything you need to conduct a thorough inspection of your new build home in England, Scotland, or Wales. It covers every area a professional snagging surveyor would inspect — so you can do it yourself for a fraction of the cost.

## What Is Snagging?

In the UK, "snagging" refers to the process of identifying defects, incomplete work, and poor workmanship in a new build property. A snag list (or snagging list) is the documented record of everything that needs attention before or after you take possession.

Under the NHBC Buildmark warranty — the most common warranty scheme for UK new builds — your builder is responsible for rectifying defects reported in writing during the first two years. The earlier you catch and report them, the better your chances of getting them fixed promptly.

## When to Snag Your New Build

The best time is **before legal completion** — before you transfer the final payment. Under consumer protection regulations in the UK, you have the right to request a pre-completion inspection. Many builders will allow this if asked.

If you're only getting access at key handover, do your inspection on that day and submit your list within 7 days.

You can also submit defects at any time during the NHBC Buildmark's 2-year builder liability period — so even if you miss something at first, you can still raise it later.

## External Checks

Walk the full perimeter of the property before entering.

1. **Brickwork:** Look for cracked, missing, or poorly pointed mortar joints. Check that courses are level and consistent. Efflorescence (white salt staining) on brickwork indicates moisture issues.
2. **Roof:** From the ground, check ridge tiles are straight and securely pointed. No cracked or slipped tiles. Flashings around any chimneys or dormers should be neatly dressed.
3. **Gutters and downpipes:** Gutters should be level or slightly sloped to direct water to the downpipe. Check all fixings are secure. No gaps at joints.
4. **Windows and external doors:** Every external window should open, close, and lock correctly. Weatherstripping should be continuous with no gaps. UPVC frames should be clean with no scratches or deformation.
5. **Driveway and paths:** No cracks, trip hazards, or hollow-sounding slabs. Drainage should slope away from the house. Block paving should be consistent with no sunken areas.
6. **Garden:** Boundary fences and walls should be plumb and securely fixed. Turf or seed covering should be even. Check the ground level is adequate — soil should not be banked against air bricks.

## Hall and Stairs

1. Front door should swing freely and the latch engage cleanly. Draught seals should be intact.
2. Stairs: each tread should be level and secure (no movement or squeaks). Balusters should be firmly fixed — test each one. Handrail should not move.
3. Plasterwork: check for cracks at wall junctions, door frames, and ceiling edges.
4. Skirting boards and architraves: joints should be tight, faces flat against the wall.
5. Test every light switch and socket.

## Living Room and Dining Room

1. Test all sockets — use a socket tester for accuracy.
2. Check coving or cornicing for gaps at corners and wall/ceiling junctions.
3. Inspect the chimney breast or media wall if present — check for cracking or poor finishing around recesses.
4. In natural light, look at wall surfaces at a raking angle to spot poor skim coat finish.
5. Check any bay windows for draught — hold your hand near the frame on a breezy day.
6. Check flooring for lippage, gaps, and damage.

## Kitchen

1. Open every cabinet door and drawer — all should open smoothly and align perfectly when closed.
2. Check handles are all at the same height and fully tightened.
3. Worktop joints at mitres should be tight with no step or gap.
4. Inspect splashback tiling for alignment, consistent grout width, and complete silicon sealing at worktop edge.
5. Run the cold then hot tap — hot water should arrive within 30 seconds. Check immediately under the sink for any moisture.
6. Test the hob, oven, and extractor (if fitted). Extractor should extract effectively and not rattle.
7. Check the dishwasher connection and run a quick cycle if possible.
8. Inspect plinth boards for level and consistent fixing.

## Master Bedroom and Bedrooms

1. Open and close built-in wardrobes — doors should be plumb, hinges adjusted, and sliding mechanisms smooth.
2. Test every socket and check positions match specification.
3. Check radiators heat fully. Cold spots indicate an airlock.
4. Bedroom windows should have restrictive opening latch (required by building regulations above ground floor level).
5. Check under windowsills for condensation staining — a sign of cold bridging.
6. Inspect ceiling for paint uniformity — uneven coverage shows as shadows in natural light.

## Bathrooms and En-Suites

1. Check all tiling: alignment, lippage (uneven tile edges), consistent grout width. Look for hollow tiles by tapping firmly.
2. Silicon sealant joints at bath/basin/shower tray edges should be continuous and neatly finished.
3. Flush every toilet and watch for slow cistern refill (over 90 seconds indicates a problem).
4. Test shower pressure and drainage. The tray should empty within 60 seconds.
5. Shower screens should open and close smoothly, and seals should be intact.
6. Check bathroom fan operates and continues to run after the light is switched off (required by building regulations).
7. Inspect chrome fittings for scratches and ensure all fixings are tight.

## Windows Throughout

Check every window in the property:
1. Opens and closes smoothly.
2. Locks securely with key.
3. No condensation between panes (double glazing failure).
4. Draught seals are continuous.
5. Restrictive latch operates correctly on upper floor windows.

## Roof Space / Loft

If accessible:
1. Insulation should cover the entire floor area — minimum 270mm of mineral wool.
2. No signs of moisture, staining, or daylight visible through the roof covering.
3. Ventilation is present — soffit vents must be clear.
4. Any water storage tank must be insulated.
5. Check that any MVHR or mechanical ventilation units are correctly installed.

## Heating and Hot Water

1. Run the boiler and heating system for a full cycle. Check every radiator heats fully.
2. Boiler pressure gauge should read 1–1.5 bar cold.
3. Programmer and thermostat should control zones as described in handover documents.
4. If there's an unvented hot water cylinder, check the pressure relief valve is correctly positioned over a tundish.
5. Test the hot water — it should reach 60°C at the cylinder (legionella prevention requirement).

## 5 Quick Wins for Your UK Snagging Inspection

1. **Bring a socket tester.** Available at any DIY store for £5–£8. Tests wiring polarity without any electrical knowledge.
2. **Photograph in portrait.** Vertical photos show the full height of walls and doors — better for context than landscape.
3. **Test everything twice.** A tap might not leak on the first run but show a drip on the second.
4. **Check on a rainy day.** Nothing reveals roof and window defects like sustained rainfall. Return on a wet day and recheck.
5. **Note item numbers.** Builders respond faster when they can reference "item 23" rather than searching your description.

## Your NHBC Rights in Summary

- **Years 1–2:** Your builder must fix all defects you report in writing.
- **Years 3–10:** NHBC covers structural defects if your builder can't or won't repair them.
- **Key rule:** Always report in writing — verbal reports are not sufficient for warranty claims.
