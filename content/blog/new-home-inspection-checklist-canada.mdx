Taking possession of a new build home in Canada is a significant milestone — and one that comes with a formal process designed specifically to protect your interests. Whether you're buying in Ontario (where the Tarion warranty scheme applies), British Columbia, Alberta, or another province, you have the right to inspect your home before signing off on possession.

This guide walks you through what to check, how to document what you find, and how to protect your rights under Canada's various new home warranty programs.

## The Pre-Delivery Inspection (PDI)

In Ontario, the **Pre-Delivery Inspection (PDI)** is a mandatory walkthrough conducted with a representative from your builder before possession. It's your opportunity to identify defects, incomplete work, and items that don't match the specification you agreed to.

The PDI produces a PDI form — a formal document signed by both you and the builder representative — that records all items noted during the inspection. This document is extremely important: it's your official record of the home's condition at possession and forms part of your Tarion warranty claim record.

**Critical rule:** Even if something looks fine during the PDI, you can still add it to your 30-day list or year-end list under Tarion if a problem develops later. The PDI is not your only chance.

Outside Ontario, similar pre-possession inspections are standard practice under other provincial warranty programs (BC's Homeowner Protection Act, Alberta New Home Warranty, etc.).

## What to Bring to Your PDI

- **SnapSnag or a physical checklist** — to guide you systematically
- **Phone or camera** — photograph every defect and the overall condition of every room
- **Power point/outlet tester** — available at Canadian Tire for under $15
- **Spirit level** — for checking tiles, cabinetry, and skirting
- **Torch** — for attic access, under-sink areas, and mechanical rooms
- **Your building contract and feature selections** — to verify what was agreed is what's been delivered
- **A copy of the builder's floor plan** — so you can verify room dimensions and window positions match what was sold

## Room-by-Room PDI Checklist

### Exterior

**Foundation and Structure:**
- Inspect visible foundation walls for cracks. Hairline cracks in poured concrete are common; horizontal or step cracks require investigation.
- Grading must slope away from the foundation — minimum 2% slope over the first 1.5m. In Canadian winters, inadequate grading causes freeze-thaw cycling against the foundation.
- Check window wells (if present) are correctly drained.

**Roof:**
- From ground level, check shingles are aligned and no tabs are lifted or cracked.
- Flashings at all roof penetrations (plumbing stacks, exhaust vents) should be properly sealed.
- Eaves and soffits should be complete and well-painted.

**Windows, Doors, and Exterior:**
- Open and close every external window and door. All should operate smoothly and lock securely.
- Check weatherstripping is complete — especially important for Canadian winters.
- External caulking around all window and door frames must be continuous. Gaps cause air leakage and water infiltration — critical in freeze-thaw climates.
- Check hose bibs (exterior taps) — they should have a frost-free shutoff.

**Garage:**
- Test the garage door opener — both directions. Verify the auto-reverse safety function.
- Check the door from garage to living space is a fire-rated door with self-closing hardware (required by Ontario Building Code and most provincial codes).
- Check for any cracks in the garage slab.

### Hall and Main Living Areas

- Test every outlet with a tester — check for correct wiring.
- Test every light switch — confirm it operates the correct fixture.
- Inspect walls and ceilings in natural light for paint uniformity, nail pops, and cracks at corners.
- Interior doors should swing freely and latch on first close.
- Skirting boards should be flush against the wall with tight mitre joints.
- Hardwood or laminate floors should lie flat with no gaps or cupped boards.

### Kitchen

- Open and close every cabinet door and drawer. Doors must align and close fully; drawers must not sag or jump off runners.
- Check countertop mitre joints — flush with no visible step or gap.
- Inspect backsplash tiling for consistent grout lines and silicon seal at countertop edge.
- Run the kitchen tap — check hot water arrives and inspect under the sink for leaks.
- Test the range exhaust fan on all settings.
- Test appliances if included — dishwasher, range, microwave.
- Check cabinet handles are uniformly installed and fully tightened.

### Bathrooms

- Inspect all tiling for lippage, cracked tiles, and consistent grout. Tap each tile — hollow sound indicates poor adhesion.
- Silicon sealant at tub, shower tray, and basin edges must be continuous.
- Flush every toilet — cistern should refill within 90 seconds.
- Run every shower — check water pressure, temperature, and drainage speed.
- Test exhaust fans — they must run and duct to the exterior (not the attic).
- Check all chrome fixtures for scratches and secure fixing.

### Bedrooms

- Test every outlet and verify positions match the specification.
- Open and close closet doors — sliding doors should glide without lifting out of track.
- Check bedroom door hardware — latch and lock should operate correctly.
- Verify windows open, close, and lock. Restrictive latches required above ground level.

### Basement

- Check walls for efflorescence (white salt staining — indicates moisture intrusion).
- Inspect the floor slab for cracks. Small hairline cracks are normal; cracks with heave or moisture are not.
- Verify the sump pump operates — pour a bucket of water into the pit.
- Check any rough-in plumbing is correctly installed and capped.
- Inspect window wells — they must drain freely and not be subject to water pooling.

### HRV/ERV System

Heat Recovery Ventilators (HRV) or Energy Recovery Ventilators (ERV) are required in most new Canadian homes under the National Building Code.

- Verify the HRV/ERV is installed and commissioned.
- Test operation — you should feel fresh air coming in through the supply grilles.
- Check the filter is accessible and documented in the handover package.
- Confirm the HRV is balanced — intake and exhaust should be approximately equal.

### Heating System

- Test the furnace in heat mode. All supply vents should deliver warm air within 5 minutes.
- Check the thermostat operates correctly and displays the correct temperature.
- Verify the air filter is installed and accessible.
- If radiant in-floor heating is present, test every zone.
- Check the hot water heater — verify it is set to 60°C (Canadian plumbing code requirement for legionella prevention).

### Attic

If safely accessible:
- Insulation should cover the entire floor area — minimum R-60 recommended for most Canadian climates.
- No signs of moisture, frost staining (in winter), or daylight through the roof deck.
- HRV or exhaust fan ducts must terminate at the exterior, not in the attic.
- Soffit vents must be clear of insulation — proper ventilation prevents ice dam formation.

## 5 Critical Checks for Canadian New Builds

1. **Exterior caulking and weatherstripping.** Canada's freeze-thaw climate is brutal on gaps. Every penetration in the building envelope must be sealed. Check every window and door frame, all pipe and wire penetrations.

2. **HRV operation.** The HRV is mandatory in most new Canadian homes and is often not properly commissioned. Verify it's running and that the filters are accessible. Poor HRV operation causes indoor air quality problems and excess humidity.

3. **Sump pump.** Many Canadian homes have a sump pump to manage groundwater. It must be tested — pour a bucket of water in the pit. If it doesn't activate, that's a warranty item.

4. **Insulation depth.** Canada requires far higher insulation levels than most other countries due to the climate. Request documentation of the insulation R-value installed and check the attic coverage yourself.

5. **Garage-to-house fire separation.** Every door between an attached garage and the living space must be a fire-rated door with a self-closing mechanism. Check the door label and test that it closes automatically.

## After the PDI: Your Tarion Timelines (Ontario)

- **PDI:** Document all defects on the PDI form
- **30-Day List:** Submit within 30 days of possession (for items missed at PDI or that appeared shortly after)
- **Year-End List:** Submit between days 270–365 of possession
- **Second Year List:** Submit between months 18–24 for defects appearing in year 2

Each list submission triggers an obligation on your builder to respond and repair.
